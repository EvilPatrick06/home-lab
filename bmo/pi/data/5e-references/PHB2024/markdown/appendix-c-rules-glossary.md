# Appendix C: Rules Glossary

This appendix is a glossary of rules — definitions of game terms and quick explanations of rules that are referenced often in the game. The glossary entries are organized alphabetically and are meant to be read when you need to understand a term or rule.

---

**Ability Check.** An ability check is a D20 Test that represents using one of the six abilities — or a specific skill associated with an ability — to overcome a challenge. The DM calls for an ability check when a character or monster attempts an action (other than an attack) that has a chance of failure. The DM decides which ability is relevant to the attempt and the difficulty of the task, represented by a DC.

**Ability Score and Modifier.** Each creature has six ability scores — Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma — each ranging from 1 to 20 for most adventurers. Each ability also has a modifier, derived from the score: (score - 10) / 2, rounded down. The modifier is what you add to a D20 Test.

**Action.** On each of your turns during combat, you can move and take one action. The Actions table lists the game's main actions.

### Actions

| Action | Summary |
|---|---|
| Attack | Attack with a weapon or Unarmed Strike |
| Dash | For the rest of the turn, give yourself extra movement equal to your Speed |
| Disengage | Your movement doesn't provoke Opportunity Attacks for the rest of the turn |
| Dodge | Until the start of your next turn, attack rolls against you have Disadvantage, and you make Dexterity saving throws with Advantage. You lose this benefit if you have the Incapacitated condition or if your Speed is 0 |
| Help | Help another creature's ability check or attack roll, or administer First Aid |
| Hide | Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check |
| Influence | Make a Charisma (Deception, Intimidation, Performance, or Persuasion) or Wisdom (Animal Handling) check to alter a creature's attitude |
| Magic | Cast a spell, use a magic item, or use a magical feature |
| Ready | Prepare to take an action in response to a trigger you define |
| Search | Make a Wisdom (Insight, Medicine, Perception, or Survival) check |
| Study | Make an Intelligence (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, or Religion) check |
| Utilize | Use a nonmagical object |

**Advantage.** If you have Advantage on a D20 Test, roll two d20s and use the higher roll. A roll can't benefit from more than one source of Advantage, and Advantage and Disadvantage on the same roll cancel each other, regardless of the number of each.

**Adventure.** An adventure is a series of connected encounters that form a cohesive story.

**Alignment.** A creature's alignment broadly describes its ethical attitudes and ideals. Alignment is a combination of two factors: one identifies morality (Good, Evil, or Neutral), and the other describes attitudes toward society and order (Lawful, Chaotic, or Neutral). These factors create nine possible alignments.

**Area of Effect.** Areas of effect define the areas where spells and other features take effect. The shapes are defined here:

- **Cone.** A Cone extends in a direction you choose from its point of origin. A Cone's width at a given point along its length is equal to that point's distance from the point of origin. A Cone's point of origin isn't included in the Cone's area of effect unless you decide otherwise.
- **Cube.** You select a Cube's point of origin, which lies anywhere on a face of the Cube. The Cube's size expresses the length of each side. A Cube's point of origin isn't included in the Cube's area of effect unless you decide otherwise.
- **Cylinder.** A Cylinder's point of origin is the center of either the top or bottom of the Cylinder. The Cylinder's size expresses the radius and height. A Cylinder's point of origin is included in the Cylinder's area of effect.
- **Emanation.** An Emanation originates from a creature or a point in space, extending outward in all directions. The Emanation's size expresses the distance it extends. An Emanation's point of origin (creature or point) is included in the Emanation's area of effect.
- **Line.** A Line extends from its point of origin in a straight path along its length and covers an area defined by its width. A Line's point of origin isn't included in the Line's area of effect unless you decide otherwise.
- **Sphere.** You select a Sphere's point of origin, and the Sphere extends outward from that point. The Sphere's size expresses the radius. A Sphere's point of origin is included in the Sphere's area of effect.

**Armor Class (AC).** An Armor Class is the target number for an attack roll. AC represents how difficult it is to hit a target. Armor, shields, spells, and other factors can grant a bonus to AC or set AC to a specific number.

**Armor Training.** If you wear armor without having training with it, you have Disadvantage on any D20 Test that uses Strength or Dexterity, and you can't cast spells.

**Attack Roll.** An attack roll is a D20 Test that represents making an attack. Attack rolls are made against a target's AC.

**Attitude.** A monster has a starting attitude toward a player character: Friendly, Hostile, or Indifferent.

**Attunement.** Some magic items require a creature to form a bond — called Attunement — with them before the creature can use the item's magical properties. A creature can be attuned to no more than three magic items at a time. Attuning to an item requires a Short Rest focused on only that item while in physical contact with it. An item can be attuned to only one creature at a time.

**Blindsight.** If you have Blindsight, you can see within a specific range without relying on physical sight. Within that range, you can see anything that isn't behind Total Cover, even if you have the Blinded condition or are in Darkness. Moreover, in that range you can see something that has the Invisible condition.

**Bloodied.** A creature is Bloodied while it has half its Hit Points or fewer remaining.

**Bonus Action.** On your turn, you can take one Bonus Action if a spell, feature, or other rule allows it. You choose when to take a Bonus Action during your turn unless the Bonus Action's timing is specified.

**Breaking Objects.** When you take the Attack action, you can use your attack to try to destroy an object.

**Campaign.** A campaign is a series of adventures that form an even bigger, overarching story.

**Cantrip.** A cantrip is a spell that can be cast at will, without using a spell slot and without being prepared. Repeated practice has fixed the spell in the caster's mind.

**Carrying Capacity.** Your carrying capacity is your Strength score multiplied by 15. That is the weight in pounds you can carry. If you carry weight in excess of your carrying capacity, your Speed drops to 5 feet. You can't carry weight in excess of twice your carrying capacity.

**Challenge Rating (CR).** Challenge Rating is a way of measuring the difficulty of a monster. A monster's CR tells the DM how many characters of a certain level the monster is expected to challenge.

**Character Sheet.** A character sheet is the document on which you track your character's information.

**Charm.** Some abilities and spells can Charm a creature. As defined in the Charmed condition, a Charmed creature can't attack or target its charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects, and the charmer has Advantage on ability checks to interact socially with the Charmed creature.

**Climbing.** While climbing, each foot of movement costs 1 extra foot (2 extra feet in Difficult Terrain). At the DM's option, climbing a slippery surface requires a successful DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check.

**Concentration.** Some spells and other effects require Concentration. The effect ends if the concentrator has the Incapacitated condition, dies, or does something else that breaks their Concentration (such as casting another spell requiring Concentration). If a concentrator takes damage, they must succeed on a Constitution saving throw, or their Concentration is broken. The DC is 10 or half the damage taken (round down), whichever is higher.

**Condition.** A condition is a temporary game state. The definition of a condition says how it affects its recipient.

## Conditions

**Blinded.** A Blinded creature can't see and automatically fails any ability check that requires sight. Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage, and the creature's attack rolls have Disadvantage.

**Charmed.** A Charmed creature can't attack the charmer or target the charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects. The charmer has Advantage on any ability check to interact socially with the creature.

**Deafened.** A Deafened creature can't hear and automatically fails any ability check that requires hearing.

**Exhaustion.** While you have the Exhaustion condition, you experience the following effects:

- **Exhaustion Levels.** This condition is cumulative. Each time you receive it, you gain 1 Exhaustion level. You die if your Exhaustion level is 6.
- **D20 Tests Affected.** When you make a D20 Test, the roll is reduced by 2 times your Exhaustion level.
- **Speed Reduced.** Your Speed is reduced by a number of feet equal to 5 times your Exhaustion level.
- **Removing Exhaustion Levels.** Finishing a Long Rest removes 1 of your Exhaustion levels. When your Exhaustion level reaches 0, the condition ends.

**Frightened.** A Frightened creature has Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of its fear is within line of sight. The creature can't willingly move closer to the source of its fear.

**Grappled.** A Grappled creature's Speed is 0 and can't increase. The condition ends if the grappler has the Incapacitated condition or if the distance between the Grappled creature and the grappler exceeds the grapple's range.

**Incapacitated.** An Incapacitated creature can't take actions or Reactions.

**Invisible.** An Invisible creature is impossible to see without the aid of magic or a special sense. The creature is Heavily Obscured. The creature's location can be detected by any noise it makes or any tracks it leaves. Attack rolls against the creature have Disadvantage, and the creature's attack rolls have Advantage.

**Paralyzed.** A Paralyzed creature has the Incapacitated condition. The creature can't move or speak. The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws. Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage. Any attack that hits the creature is a Critical Hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature.

**Petrified.** A Petrified creature is transformed, along with any nonmagical object it is wearing or carrying, into a solid inanimate substance (usually stone). Its weight increases by a factor of ten, and it ceases aging. The creature has the Incapacitated condition. The creature can't move or speak and is unaware of its surroundings. Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage. The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws. The creature has Resistance to all damage. The creature has Immunity to Poison and the Poisoned condition, although a poison already in its system is suspended, not neutralized.

**Poisoned.** A Poisoned creature has Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.

**Prone.** A Prone creature's only movement option is to crawl, unless it stands up. The creature has Disadvantage on attack rolls. An attack roll against the creature has Advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet; otherwise, the attack roll has Disadvantage.

**Restrained.** A Restrained creature's Speed is 0 and can't increase. Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage. The creature's attack rolls have Disadvantage. The creature has Disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws.

**Stunned.** A Stunned creature has the Incapacitated condition. The creature can't move and can speak only falteringly. The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws. Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage.

**Unconscious.** An Unconscious creature has the Incapacitated condition. The creature can't move or speak and is unaware of its surroundings. The creature drops whatever it's holding and has the Prone condition. The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws. Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage. Any attack that hits the creature is a Critical Hit if the attacker is within 5 feet.

**Cover.** A target has cover if it is behind something that can block attacks.

- **Half Cover.** +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws.
- **Three-Quarters Cover.** +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws.
- **Total Cover.** Can't be targeted directly by an attack or a spell, although some spells can reach such a target by including it in an area of effect.

**Crawling.** While crawling, each foot of movement costs 1 extra foot (2 extra feet in Difficult Terrain).

**Creature.** Any being in the game, including a player's character, is a creature.

**Creature Type.** Every creature, including every player character, has a tag that identifies the kind of creature it is. Most player characters are of the Humanoid type. The creature types are: Aberration, Beast, Celestial, Construct, Dragon, Elemental, Fey, Fiend, Giant, Humanoid, Monstrosity, Ooze, Plant, and Undead. These types don't have rules themselves, but some rules in the game affect creatures of certain types in different ways.

**Critical Hit.** If you roll a 20 on the d20 for an attack roll, the attack hits regardless of any modifiers or the target's AC. This is called a Critical Hit. When you score a Critical Hit, you roll all of the attack's damage dice twice and add them together. Then add any relevant modifiers as normal.

**D20 Test.** Any time a character or monster attempts something that has a chance of failure, it makes a D20 Test by rolling a d20 and adding any applicable modifiers. The three types are ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws.

**Damage.** Damage represents harm caused by attacks, spells, and other effects. It reduces Hit Points.

### Damage Types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Acid | Corrosive acids |
| Bludgeoning | Blunt force |
| Cold | Freezing cold |
| Fire | Flames and extreme heat |
| Force | Pure magical energy |
| Lightning | Electrical energy |
| Necrotic | Life-draining energy |
| Piercing | Puncturing and impaling |
| Poison | Toxins and venom |
| Psychic | Mental energy |
| Radiant | Holy and divine energy |
| Slashing | Cuts and lacerations |
| Thunder | Concussive sound |

**Damage Threshold.** A creature or object has Immunity to all damage unless it takes an amount of damage from a single attack or effect equal to or greater than its damage threshold, in which case it takes that damage.

**Darkvision.** If you have Darkvision, you can see in Dim Light within a specified range as if it were Bright Light and in Darkness within that range as if it were Dim Light. You discern colors in that Darkness only as shades of gray.

**Dead.** A creature is dead if it has 0 Hit Points and has not been stabilized. Death can be reversed by certain spells, like *Revivify*.

**Death Saving Throw.** When you start your turn with 0 Hit Points, you make a special saving throw called a Death Saving Throw. Roll a d20. On a 10 or higher, you succeed. Otherwise, you fail. Three successes: you become Stable. Three failures: you die. Rolling a 1 counts as two failures. Rolling a 20 means you regain 1 HP. Damage at 0 HP causes a failed Death Saving Throw (or two if from a Critical Hit).

**Dehydration.** A creature requires an amount of water per day based on its size. A creature that drinks less than half the required water must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or gain 1 Exhaustion level at the end of the day. A creature with access to even less water automatically gains 1 Exhaustion level.

**Difficult Terrain.** If a space is Difficult Terrain, every foot of movement in that space costs 1 extra foot.

**Difficulty Class (DC).** A Difficulty Class is the target number for an ability check or saving throw. It represents the difficulty of a task.

**Disadvantage.** If you have Disadvantage on a D20 Test, roll two d20s and use the lower roll. A roll can't be affected by more than one source of Disadvantage, and Advantage and Disadvantage on the same roll cancel each other, regardless of the number of each.

**Encounter.** An encounter is a scene in an adventure that is defined by an interesting challenge, like a battle, a social negotiation, or a dangerous hazard.

**Experience Points (XP).** Experience Points are a measure of a character's progress toward gaining a level. When a character earns enough XP, the character advances in level.

**Falling.** A creature falls when there is nothing holding it up. A falling creature drops up to 500 feet per turn. At the end of a fall, a creature takes 1d6 Bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet it fell, to a maximum of 20d6. The creature has the Prone condition when it lands, unless it avoids taking damage from the fall.

**Flying.** If a flying creature has the Incapacitated or Prone condition or its Fly Speed is reduced to 0, the creature falls unless it can hover or is being held aloft by magic.

**Food.** A creature requires food each day based on its size (see Water/Food Needs per Day table). A creature that eats but consumes less than half the required food must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw at the end of the day or gain 1 Exhaustion level. A creature that eats nothing automatically gains 1 Exhaustion level at the end of the day.

**Grappling.** When you take the Attack action, you can replace one of your attacks with a grapple attempt. The target must be no more than one size larger than you, and it must be within your reach. You make a Strength (Athletics) check against the target's escape DC (8 + its Strength modifier + its Proficiency Bonus). On a success, the target has the Grappled condition.

**Heavily Obscured.** You have the Blinded condition while trying to see something in a Heavily Obscured area. A Heavily Obscured area — such as Darkness, thick fog, or dense foliage — blocks vision entirely.

**Heroic Inspiration.** If you have Heroic Inspiration, you can expend it when you make a D20 Test to give yourself Advantage on the roll. You either have Heroic Inspiration or you don't; you can't stockpile multiple instances. If you have it when you gain it, you can give it to another character in your group.

**Hit Point Dice (Hit Dice).** A character's Hit Dice are determined by class. Roll your Hit Die + your Constitution modifier to determine how many Hit Points you regain when you spend a Hit Die during a Short Rest.

**Hit Points (HP).** Hit Points represent a combination of physical and mental durability, the will to live, and luck. Creatures with more Hit Points are harder to kill. A creature's current Hit Points can be any number from the creature's Hit Point maximum down to 0.

**Hostile.** A Hostile creature views you as a foe.

**Immunity.** If you have Immunity to a damage type, damage of that type doesn't reduce your Hit Points. If you have Immunity to a condition, you aren't affected by it.

**Indifferent.** An Indifferent creature has no strong feelings about you one way or another.

**Influence.** The Influence action lets you use a Charisma check (Deception, Intimidation, Performance, or Persuasion) or Wisdom (Animal Handling) check to shift a creature's attitude.

**Initiative.** Initiative determines the order of turns during combat. When combat starts, every participant makes a Dexterity check to determine their place in the Initiative order. The DM makes one roll for an entire group of identical creatures.

**Jumping.** When you take the Jump action, you make a Strength (Athletics) check and cover a number of feet up to the check's total (Long Jump) or one-quarter that total (High Jump, minimum 1 foot). Each foot cleared on a jump costs a foot of movement. If you spend no movement before jumping, the distances are halved. If you land in Difficult Terrain, you must make a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or have the Prone condition.

**Knocking a Creature Out.** When an attacker reduces a creature to 0 HP with a melee attack, the attacker can knock the creature out, leaving it with the Unconscious condition instead of killing it.

**Lightly Obscured.** You have Disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks to see something in a Lightly Obscured area. A Lightly Obscured area — such as Dim Light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage — makes details harder to see.

**Light.** Three categories of illumination:

- **Bright Light.** Lets most creatures see normally.
- **Dim Light.** Creates a Lightly Obscured area.
- **Darkness.** Creates a Heavily Obscured area.

**Line of Sight.** To target something, you must have a clear path to it, so it can't be behind Total Cover.

**Long Rest.** A Long Rest is a period of extended downtime — at least 8 hours — during which you sleep for at least 6 hours and perform no more than 2 hours of light activity. You can't benefit from more than one Long Rest in a 24-hour period. At the end of a Long Rest, you regain all lost Hit Points and spent Hit Point Dice (up to a number equal to half your total Hit Dice, minimum of one die).

**Melee Attack.** A melee attack is an attack made against a target within reach (usually 5 ft.). It can be a melee weapon attack or a melee spell attack.

**Mounted Combat.** A willing creature at least one size larger than you that has an appropriate anatomy can serve as a mount. While mounted, you can choose whether the mount acts independently or you control it. A controlled mount shares your Initiative count and can only take the Dash, Disengage, or Dodge action.

**Multiclassing.** Multiclassing lets you gain levels in multiple classes. You must meet the ability score prerequisites for both your current class and the new class.

**Opportunity Attack.** You can make an Opportunity Attack when a creature you can see leaves your reach using its movement, action, or Reaction. You use your Reaction to make one melee attack against the provoking creature. The attack occurs right before the creature leaves your reach.

**Passive Perception.** Passive Perception = 10 + the creature's Wisdom (Perception) modifier. Advantage adds 5; Disadvantage subtracts 5.

**Per Day.** A game feature that can be used a certain number of times per day regains all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

**Proficiency Bonus.** A Proficiency Bonus is added to D20 Tests you're proficient in and to your spell save DC. It is determined by your level. You never add your Proficiency Bonus more than once to the same roll.

**Proficiency.** If you have proficiency with something — a weapon, tool, skill, saving throw, etc. — you can add your Proficiency Bonus to relevant D20 Tests. You gain proficiencies from your species, background, class, and feats.

**Ranged Attack.** A ranged attack is an attack made against a target at a distance using a ranged weapon or spell. You have Disadvantage on a ranged attack roll if you are within 5 feet of a Hostile creature that can see you and doesn't have the Incapacitated condition.

**Reaction.** A Reaction is an instant response to a trigger. You can take one Reaction per round — you regain your Reaction at the start of each of your turns.

**Resistance.** If you have Resistance to a damage type, damage of that type is halved against you (round down). Resistance applies after all other modifiers to damage. Multiple instances of Resistance don't stack.

**Ritual.** If a spell has the Ritual tag, you can cast it as a Ritual. Casting a spell as a Ritual adds 10 minutes to its casting time, and the spell doesn't expend a spell slot.

**Round.** A round represents about 6 seconds in the game world, during which every participant in combat takes a turn.

**Saving Throw.** A saving throw — also called a save — is a D20 Test that represents an attempt to resist or avoid a threat. You don't normally decide to make a saving throw; you are forced to make one because your character or monster is at risk of harm.

**Search.** The Search action lets you make a Wisdom check (Insight, Medicine, Perception, or Survival) to find or discern something.

### Areas of Knowledge (Study Action)

| Skill | Areas |
|---|---|
| Arcana | Spells, magic items, eldritch symbols, magical traditions, planes of existence, certain creatures (Aberrations, Constructs, Elementals, Fey, Monstrosities) |
| History | Historic events and people, ancient civilizations, wars, legends, cultural customs |
| Investigation | Traps, ciphers, riddles, gadgetry |

| Religion | Deities, holy symbols, sacred rites, religious hierarchies, certain creatures (Celestials, Fiends, Undead) |

**Session.** A session is a single meeting of the D&D group.

**Short Rest.** A Short Rest is a 1-hour period of downtime, during which a character does nothing more strenuous than eating, drinking, reading, and tending to wounds. During a Short Rest, a character can spend Hit Dice to regain Hit Points. Roll each spent Hit Die and add the character's Constitution modifier to determine the Hit Points regained.

**Size.** A creature belongs to a size category: Tiny, Small, Medium, Large, Huge, or Gargantuan.

| Size | Space |
|---|---|
| Tiny | 2.5 by 2.5 ft. |
| Small | 5 by 5 ft. |
| Medium | 5 by 5 ft. |
| Large | 10 by 10 ft. |
| Huge | 15 by 15 ft. |
| Gargantuan | 20 by 20 ft. or larger |

**Skill.** A skill represents a specific aspect of an ability score that a character can be proficient in:

- **Strength:** Athletics
- **Dexterity:** Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- **Intelligence:** Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion
- **Wisdom:** Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival
- **Charisma:** Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion

**Speed.** Every creature has a Speed, which is the distance in feet that the creature can walk in 1 turn. Special movement types: Climb Speed, Fly Speed, Swim Speed, and Burrow Speed.

**Spell Attack.** A spell attack is a special attack: d20 + spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus against the target's AC.

**Spell Save DC.** Spell save DC = 8 + spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus.

**Spell Slot.** A spell slot represents a character's capacity to cast a spell. Casting a spell of level 1 or higher expends a slot of the spell's level or higher. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a Long Rest.

**Spellcasting Focus.** A Spellcasting Focus is a special item — such as a crystal, wand, or holy symbol — that you can use in place of any Material component that lacks a cost and that isn't consumed.

**Stable.** A creature is Stable if it has 0 Hit Points but isn't required to make Death Saving Throws.

**Suffocation.** A creature can hold its breath for a number of minutes equal to 1 + its Constitution modifier (minimum of 30 seconds). When a creature runs out of breath or is choking, it can survive for a number of rounds equal to its Constitution modifier (minimum of 1 round). At the start of its next turn, it drops to 0 HP and is dying, and it can't regain HP or be stabilized until it can breathe again.

**Surprise.** If combat starts and a combatant is surprised, that combatant can't move or take actions (including Bonus Actions and Reactions) on its first turn in combat, and can't take a Reaction until that turn ends.

**Swimming.** While swimming, each foot of movement costs 1 extra foot (2 extra feet in Difficult Terrain) unless a creature has a Swim Speed.

**Telepathy.** Telepathy is a magical ability that allows a creature to communicate mentally with another creature within a specified range. The contacted creature doesn't need to share a language with the telepath, but the contacted creature must understand at least one language.

**Temporary Hit Points.** Temporary Hit Points are a buffer against damage. When you take damage, Temporary HP are lost first. They can't be restored by healing, and they don't stack (you keep the higher amount). They go away after a Long Rest.

**Tremorsense.** A creature with Tremorsense can pinpoint the location of creatures and moving objects within a specific range, provided that the creature with Tremorsense and anything it's detecting are both in contact with the same surface or the same liquid.

**Truesight.** If you have Truesight, your vision is enhanced within a specified range. In that range, your vision pierces through Darkness (including magical Darkness), Illusions, invisibility, and transmutations. You also see into the Ethereal Plane within that range.

**Turn.** During a round, each participant takes a turn. On your turn, you can move a distance up to your Speed and take one action.

**Unarmed Strike.** An Unarmed Strike is a melee attack that involves using your body to damage, shove, or grapple. Whenever you use your Unarmed Strike, choose one of the following options:

- **Damage.** You make an attack roll against the target. Your bonus to hit equals your Strength modifier + your Proficiency Bonus. On a hit, the target takes Bludgeoning damage equal to 1 + your Strength modifier.
- **Grapple.** The target must succeed on a Strength or Dexterity saving throw (its choice), or it has the Grappled condition. The DC equals 8 + your Strength modifier + your Proficiency Bonus. You need a free hand for the grapple.
- **Shove.** The target must succeed on a Strength or Dexterity saving throw (its choice), or you either push the target 5 feet away or cause it to have the Prone condition. The DC equals 8 + your Strength modifier + your Proficiency Bonus.

**Vulnerability.** If you have Vulnerability to a damage type, damage of that type is doubled against you. Vulnerability is applied after all other modifiers to damage.

**Weapon Attack.** A weapon attack is an attack made with a weapon — a melee weapon attack or ranged weapon attack. Bonus to hit: ability modifier (Strength for melee, Dexterity for ranged, or as specified by a weapon property like Finesse) + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient with the weapon).
