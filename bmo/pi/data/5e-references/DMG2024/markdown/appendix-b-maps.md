# Appendix B: Maps

This appendix presents fifteen maps that DMs can use for adventure locations. Each map is a full-page battle map illustration with a grid overlay and compass rose. The maps are presented without keyed descriptions, allowing the DM to populate them with encounters, treasures, traps, and NPCs as needed. All maps were illustrated by Dyson Logos.

## Barrow Crypt

*Page 365. Scale: 1 square = 5 feet.*

A two-level underground crypt built beneath a barrow mound. A cross-section inset at the top of the page shows the barrow's profile with standing stones on the hilltop and the two dungeon levels below.

### Level 1

The upper level is accessed from the surface via a passage leading up to the standing stones (marked "Up to Standing Stones" with an "S" label). The level features a complex layout of rectangular chambers and corridors carved from stone. Notable features include:

- Multiple sarcophagi or burial alcoves lining the northern chambers
- A large central chamber with a spiral staircase descending to Level 2
- A secondary stairway on the eastern side (marked "Down to Level 2")
- Numerous smaller rooms branching off the main corridors
- A rounded cave-like chamber on the eastern edge with a large circular feature (possibly a well or pit)
- Various stone coffins and burial niches throughout

### Level 2

The lower level mirrors the footprint of Level 1 but is slightly larger. It features:

- Two staircases leading back up to Level 1 (marked "Up to Level 1"), one on the eastern side and one near the center-south
- A large spiral staircase in the southern portion
- Multiple rectangular burial chambers arranged in a grid-like pattern
- Wider corridors than Level 1
- Several sarcophagi and stone coffins distributed through the rooms
- Natural cave walls surrounding the carved stone chambers

## Crossroads Village

*Page 367. Scale: 1 square = 25 feet.*

A large-scale outdoor map showing a small village built around a crossroads where two roads intersect. A river or stream runs roughly north-south through the eastern portion of the village. This map uses a larger scale than the other maps (25 feet per square instead of 5 feet), making it suitable for overland or village-scale encounters.

### Features

- **Buildings:** Approximately two to three dozen structures of varying sizes -- houses, barns, longhouses, and larger public buildings -- are scattered throughout the village on both sides of the river
- **Bridge:** A bridge crosses the river near the center of the map where the east-west road meets the waterway
- **Roads:** Two main roads form the crossroads, with one running roughly north-south and the other east-west
- **Fields:** Several cultivated fields (shown with parallel line hatching) surround the village, particularly to the west and south
- **Octagonal structure:** A prominent octagonal building (possibly a temple, town hall, or market) sits in the western portion of the village
- **Large rectangular building:** A sizable structure (possibly a manor house, inn, or guild hall) is located in the southern part of the village
- **Trees:** Scattered trees dot the village, with denser forest along the map edges to the north, west, and south
- **River:** The waterway meanders from north to south through the eastern half of the map, with the village built along both banks

## Dragon's Lair

- A stairway or passage descending to Level 2 (marked "Down to Level 2") in the central-eastern area
- A series of rectangular rooms carved into the eastern rock, separated by walls

### Level 2

The middle level, accessible from Level 1 above and Level 3 below. Features include:

- A stairway up to Level 1 (marked "Up to Level 1") on the western side

### Level 3

The deepest level of the lair. Features include:

- A stairway up to Level 2 (marked "Up to Level 2") on the eastern side
- A large circular feature (possibly a pit, pool, or dais) in the central-eastern area
- Several rectangular rooms carved from stone in the southeastern corner
- Natural cave passages and irregular walls throughout
- The most expansive natural cavern space of the three levels

## Dungeon Hideout

*Page 369. Scale: 1 square = 5 feet.*

A three-level underground complex with a surface-level entrance. This is one of the more complex maps, featuring numerous rooms and passages across all three levels.

### Surface Level

Two separate surface structures serve as entrances to the dungeon below:

- **Western structure:** A cluster of ruined or partially collapsed rooms with a stairway leading down (marked "Down")
- **Eastern structure:** A larger complex of rooms also with a stairway leading down (marked "Down")
- Natural cave passages and crevices connect the surface to the levels below

### Level 1

The first underground level features:

- Multiple staircases -- stairs leading up to the surface (marked "Up") on both the western and eastern sides, and stairs leading down (marked "Down") in two central locations
- A complex arrangement of rectangular rooms and corridors
- Several small rooms that could serve as barracks, storage, or cells
- Bookcases or shelves visible in some rooms
- A more organized, constructed layout compared to the natural caves above

### Level 2

The deepest level and the largest floor of the complex. Features include:

- Two staircases leading up to Level 1 (marked "Up"), one in the central area and one on the eastern side
- A secret exit on the far western side (marked "Secret Exit")
- Numerous rooms of varying sizes arranged in an extensive layout
- Natural cave areas blending with constructed rooms
- The most rooms of any level, suggesting this is the main living and operational area of the hideout
- Contour lines in the southeastern corner suggesting uneven terrain or a slope
